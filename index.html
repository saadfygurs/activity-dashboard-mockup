Design system — the 8 principles behind every screen ▸
Built from two inputs: field interviews (site FBP like Adèle, GCC insight lead like Taís) and best practice for financial dashboards. The same eight rules drive every screen — so the dashboard is learned once.
1 · One screen = one question. Each tab answers exactly one question, written in plain words at the top of its note. If a screen needs two questions, it becomes two screens. Kills the “everything on one page” reflex.
2 · The figure before the filter. The number is the biggest element and comes first; controls are demoted to collapsed chips. You read, then rarely adjust — not the reverse.
3 · Visible trust. “Does it reconcile?” is answered on the face of the screen (the green badge), because the real adoption blocker is confidence, not layout. Reconciliation is a feature, not a debug view.
4 · Progressive disclosure. General → specific. Big number first, drill for detail (A0 → A1 → transactional). Nothing is deleted; it moves one level down.
5 · No SAP jargon up front. Business words on the surface (Activity, site, budget). GR / DLV / TECO / GMID / LMID live behind an expert reveal or in Data integrity.
6 · One visual language. Same card, type scale, colour semantics and layout grammar everywhere. Opella green anchors the brand and structure; amber = watch, red = critical — colour is always semantic, never decorative.
7 · Smart defaults. The system pre-picks period, scope, cut-off, scenario and standard for ~95% of cases, shows them read-only so you know what you’re looking at, and lets the expert expand them. Zero buttons required, not zero buttons possible.
8 · File it, don’t trash it. Debug and control visuals (missing standards, refresh, transactional detail) move to a Data integrity page — out of the client path, still there for the team.

01 Purpose & success
One journey, general → particular, scoped per user (Row-Level Security). Around forty client pages collapse into six screens plus a technical page. Success is measured in adoption: the figure is trusted, and a site user gets it with zero onboarding. The pilot family is AV (Activity); the method and templates then carry to the other families. Fictitious figures throughout — the point is the structure.
02 The eight design principles
The rules behind every screen, from field interviews (Adèle, Taís) and financial-dashboard best practice. The dashboard is learned once, then reused.
  • 1 · One screen = one question. Each tab answers exactly one question, in plain words at the top. Two questions → two screens. Kills the "everything on one page" reflex.
  • 2 · The figure before the filter. The number is the biggest element and comes first; controls are demoted to collapsed chips. You read, then rarely adjust.
  • 3 · Visible trust. "Does it reconcile?" is answered on the face of the screen (the green badge). The adoption blocker is confidence, not layout — reconciliation is a feature, not a debug view.
  • 4 · Progressive disclosure. General → specific. Big number first, drill for detail. Nothing is deleted; it moves one level down.
  • 5 · No SAP jargon up front. Business words on the surface (Activity, site, budget). GR / DLV / TECO / GMID live behind an expert reveal or in Data integrity.
  • 6 · One visual language. Same card, type scale, colour semantics and layout everywhere. Opella green anchors structure; amber = watch, red = critical — colour is always semantic, never decorative.
  • 7 · Smart defaults. The system pre-picks period, scope, cut-off, scenario and standard for ~95% of cases, shows them read-only, and lets the expert expand. Zero buttons required, not zero buttons possible.

06 The two lenses — Value € vs Volume
The same activity, read two ways. A synced toggle on A0, A1 and A2; both shown together on A0b.
Value €the € lens
Industrial value added, volumes × standard cost. The common denominator across unlike products, and the figure that closes against the ledger. This is what MNS "sells" to ComOps at standard.
Volume uthe unit lens
The physical quantity produced. Heterogeneous units (boxes of 1,000, semi-finished) that are not summed across unlike products, so they read per site / line / machine. The PDP is expressed in volumes — the site partner needs this lens.
Design rule: the toggle switches the number, the unit, the breakdown and the definition behind the little "i" — a Volume figure never carries the Value definition. Value and volume are not proportional: a low-cost site can be high-volume, low-value. toggle to confirm with Kiki
07 The weekly review (A0b) — persona n°1's screen
Why it exists, and what it needs. The site finance partner (Adèle, Lisieux) doesn't use the dashboard today — she rebuilds this weekly in Excel from Athena.
  • The cadence is weekly. Her committee runs every Wednesday. The dashboard is monthly today — the weekly view is the gap.
  • The reference is the PDP (Plan Directeur de Production), not only budget. Both are shown; PDP first.
  • Value and volume together, not one or the other — so no toggle here, both lenses are on the page.
  • Down to the machine (resource code, from Athena / Kinaxis). Validates A1's line level and is feasible fast.
  • The dependency: the PDP is loaded monthly in Kinaxis; the weekly split is manual today (done by supply). That split is the one thing to build.
The killer move: if the dashboard outputs this weekly report, she drops Excel — and it feeds the committee directly.

Definitions
Official definitions where we have them (source: Taís / Alexia). to confirm marks what still needs the team.
Reference KPIs
Activity (AV)Added Value
The month's industrial value added (machine and labour hours turned into value), cumulative to date. The headline figure of the overview.
AV MovementCore
AV computed in Core from production volumes × standard costs. The operational view, used as the performance reference.
AV LedgerERP
AV recorded in the ERP after all entries, accruals and local adjustments. The official financial reference.
AV Gap (%)
AV Movement minus AV Ledger, as a percentage. The tie-out measure. Known drivers: cut-off (GR/DLV/TECO), ILDA (target vs actual), Atina↔Polaris articulation, PPV↔AV reclassification, local adjustments.
Estimated landing to confirm
Projected full-year activity: actual through month close, then forecast. The existence of a modeled measure is to confirm.
Variances monitored
Variance the principle
The gap between standard cost (the plan) and actual, attributed to a named cause. Everything runs at standard; the variances explain why reality differs. They are the content of A3.
Yield variance Y&S
Did the process give the expected output from the inputs? Standard says X input → Y output; if the actual yield is lower (losses, a reaction running below spec), more input is consumed per unit produced — an unfavorable yield variance. The efficiency of the transformation.
Substitution variance Y&S · mix
Did we use the planned materials, or swap one for another? When an input is replaced (e.g. a different excipient or source) versus the standard recipe, the cost impact of that changed mix is the substitution variance. exact definition to confirm with Taís / Kiki
PPVPurchase Price Variance
Difference between standard and actual purchase price of materials. A major driver of manufacturing performance (purchase-value side, read alongside AV).
Material price variance
Impact of raw-material price deviations compared to standard costs.
FX variance
Impact of exchange-rate fluctuations on production costs and purchases.
Posting types (SAP document types)
MA
Automatic posting generated by Athena.
MG
Manual posting related to interface corrections or operational adjustments.
SA
Manual finance adjustment (e.g. a PPV adjustment).
Cut-off — activity dating basis
AUTO
The system picks the right dating basis. The default; the site user never changes it.
GR · DLV · TECO
Goods receipt · delivery · technical completion dates. Different methodologies that shift how activity is dated.
EARLIEST · LATEST · CLOSE
Boundary and close dates for the period. Expert settings.
Scenarios & standards
Bud26
Budget 2026. The default basis for the “vs Budget” comparison.
RFRolling Forecast
The forecast reference. Activity always starts from volumes (actuals ≠ forecasts).
Std 25 · 26 · N
Standard cost version. Default = the current standard.
Full AV vs Step STD to confirm
A toggle in the current dashboard. What it changes exactly is a design point to pass with Taís / Kiki.
Scope & reference
Scope AUTO
Your perimeter, following the user through Row-Level Security. Site, region or group.
TM1
The company's monthly-close controlling reference (IBM). The dashboard is meant to feed it automatically.
Activity (€) vs Quantity (volume)
Activity is the valuation in euros — the common denominator. Quantity is the raw volume in units, which is heterogeneous (boxes of 1,000, semi-finished goods) and cannot be summed directly. Possibly a value/volume toggle. toggle to confirm with Kiki
GMID
Product identifier. A site's forecast is submitted GMID by GMID (product by product).
MIT · Kinaxis · Meet
Kinaxis computes the supply-chain forecast automatically; the MIT (manual input template), submitted through Meet, is where teams adjust it in delta, month by month.
PDP Plan Directeur de Production
The production plan — a site's key comparison, next to budget. Activity and volumes are read vs PDP. Loaded monthly in Kinaxis; a weekly split is done by hand today. A real reference to surface (e.g. Lisieux's weekly review).
GCC · Management Center
GCC = regional shared-service hubs (Bogotá, Budapest, Kuala Lumpur) supporting the sites. Management Center = the code name for a site, and the floor of data access.
Atina (Athena) · Polaris
Atina = the manufacturing ERP (source of AV, also the “Athena” of automatic postings). Polaris = the financial ERP. In Brazil and Mexico they are not integrated, which creates the gap; in the US they are one system (not in this model yet).
ILDA reclassification
AV should be a target value, but the system posts the actual value. The difference is reclassified to ILDA — a manual monthly journal. A real driver of the AV Gap, alongside Atina↔Polaris articulation errors and PPV↔AV reclassifications.
